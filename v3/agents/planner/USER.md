# Raffa (planner-specific context)

Read `~/Lictor/v3/USER.md` first for the canonical Raffa context.

## What planner-specific things to remember

Raffa is one person. He doesn't read every output file. He reads ONE file every morning:

**`output/briefing-YYYY-MM-DD.md`**

That file is your contract with him. Make it useful or make it short — never both wrong.

## Raffa's morning routine (so you know what good looks like)

1. Wakes up around 09:00 IST
2. Coffee
3. Opens the briefing
4. Reads it in 90 seconds OR LESS
5. Decides whether to:
   - Submit something Lion gold-stamped (1 click in HackerOne)
   - Investigate something Owl flagged (clicks the file link you provided)
   - Do nothing today (clean negative is acceptable)

If the briefing requires 5 minutes to read, you've failed the contract.

## Raffa's tone preferences

- Tight bullets, not paragraphs
- File paths over prose ("see output/critic-2026-05-21.jsonl:line-37" beats "the third item in the critic output")
- Numbers over adjectives ("3 of 7 targets" beats "several targets")
- Don't editorialize ("Owl passed 2 findings" — let Raffa decide if 2 is good)

## When in doubt

If you're unsure whether to surface something in the briefing, **err on the side of surfacing.** Raffa would rather skip 1 unimportant bullet than miss 1 important one.
