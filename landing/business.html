What we watch for

Every plan runs the full sweep across your authorized surface — these are the exposures most likely to bite a business first. We only ever look at what's already public, and every alert redacts the actual secret so it's never stored or emailed in the clear.

🔑

Exposed secrets & API keys

Live API keys, cloud credentials, and tokens sitting in public files, debug pages, or pushed to a public repo by mistake.

🗄️

Open databases & buckets

Cloud storage and databases that answer requests with no password — anyone can list files, pull backups, or read records.

💾

Downloadable backups

Database dumps and site archives (backup.zip, db.sql.gz) left in the web root — your whole database in one download.

📦

Leaked source & version control

A public .git directory or source maps that hand attackers your codebase — and the git history that often hides old secrets.

✉️

Spoofable email

Missing or weak SPF/DMARC that lets anyone forge mail from your domain for CEO-fraud and phishing.

🔗

Dangling subdomains

A subdomain still pointing at a deleted cloud resource — anyone can claim it and serve malware from your domain.

🛠️

Admin & debug surfaces

Admin consoles, phpMyAdmin, and debug pages reachable from the open internet with no password.

🧪

Exposed dev / staging

Pre-production environments left openly reachable instead of behind a VPN — usually the least-hardened thing you run.

🏥

Regulated data (PII / health / financial)

Customer records, SSNs, or card data in publicly readable files — a compliance incident, not just a bug.

How it works

Permission-based and observe-only by design. We never scan anything you haven't authorized, and we never touch or change your systems — we only look at what's already public.

You authorize your assets

Add the domains and GitHub orgs you own and confirm you own them. That consent is what tells us what we're allowed to watch — nothing else is ever touched.

Patrol watches, continuously

Our engine sweeps your authorized surface on a cadence. A newly added asset gets its first look within about a minute; everything is re-checked automatically after that.

You get told — with the secret redacted

New exposures alert the people you designate, by email (SMS and phone escalation on higher plans). Every alert is plain-English, severity-ranked, and includes how to contain and fix it. The live secret is always masked.
