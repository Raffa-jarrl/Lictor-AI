Why now

In 2024 AI talked. In 2025 it acted.
In 2026 it’s running businesses.

The security model hasn’t caught up. Vibe-coded SaaS ships with hardcoded API keys. AI agents call cross-app tools with user data. The OWASP LLM Top 10 keeps growing.

Why open source?

Every AI agent platform — from Zapier to Make to Manus — needs this. A closed enterprise product becomes one of five vendors. Open-sourced, we become the standard.

Why now (regulatory)?

The EU AI Act enters phase-2 enforcement in 2026. NIST AI RMF is being adopted by every Fortune 500. Compliance-shaped demand exists today; the supply is missing.

What’s the moat?

Network effects. Every Sentinel install adds telemetry that improves every other install. Lakera and Protect AI see their own customers. We see the union.

Coming Q1 2027 — Lictor Sentry

AI threat protection for high-value individuals. iOS VPN profile that monitors outbound AI API calls from your phone — for executives, journalists, dissidents, anyone whose AI assistant has access to sensitive data.

We’ll only email you when Sentry is ready to install. No newsletter.

Who’s building this?

Lictor is built by a 20-year cybersecurity engineer based in Israel. Prior work spans CISO advisory at Fortune 500 and security architecture for venture-backed startups. Lictor exists because AI security cannot remain an enterprise-only category — the consumer wedge and the developer wedge are both wide open, and waiting another year for the incumbents to ship a free OSS layer means the wrong companies define the category.

Lictor’s sister project, GenerationAI, teaches developers how to ship AI apps that pass Lictor’s checks.
