## The premise

By May 31, 2027, Year 1 has produced one of three observable realities:

| Reality | Definition |
|---|---|
| **A — Strong fit** | $25–50k MRR achieved + ≥5 of 7 category-defining tests passed + 1+ acquirer informational meeting + 50k+ stars |
| **B — Soft fit** | $10–25k MRR + 3–4 category tests passed + acquirer signals but no real conversations + 25k+ stars |
| **C — Weak fit** | <$10k MRR + ≤2 category tests + no acquirer signals + <15k stars |

Year 2's plan depends entirely on which reality Year 1 produced. **Don't pre-decide.** Run Year 1. Then read the signal. Then choose the Year 2 lane.

This document sketches the three lanes so the choice is faster on May 31, 2027.

## Lane A — Acquisition / hand-off path (strong fit + acquirer interest)

**Trigger:** by May 31, 2027, at least one of {Snyk, Aikido, GitHub, Anthropic, Cloudflare, Datadog} has had a substantive conversation with Dor about strategic partnership, investment, or acquisition. AND Reality A holds.

### Year 2 sequence

**Q3 2027 (Jun-Aug):**
- Engage outside M&A advisor / banker on retainer (~$15-30k for the engagement, paid out of operating cash)
- Formalize 2-3 acquirer-grade conversations into structured "would-this-shape-fit" exchanges
- Diligence-readiness audit by the advisor: what gaps need closing before a term sheet?
- DON'T solicit competing bids yet — the conversations are still informational. The advisor's job is to keep them parallel + warm without making it a process.

**Q4 2027 (Sep-Nov):**
- IF one acquirer surfaces a real number (LOI or strong informal range): the advisor activates parallel exploratory conversations with 2-3 others. NOT a formal banked process — just enough movement to anchor pricing.
- Continue normal Lictor operations — every teardown, every release, every customer signed strengthens the position. Don't enter "deal mode" prematurely.
- Public roadmap continues as if no deal is happening. The deal-mode shift is internal-only.

**Q1 2028 (Dec-Feb):**
- IF a term sheet arrives: 60-day negotiation window. Advisor + lawyer (engaged Mar 2027 per Year 1 plan) handle structure.
- Key term areas: founder vesting / retention package, Foundation independence guarantee, OSS-roadmap commitment, brand-voice survival.
- IF nothing crystallizes: defer to Q2; the position only strengthens with more months of execution.

**Q2 2028 (Mar-May):**
- Close, or close the conversation cleanly with "we appreciate it; we'll keep building." Lane A → Lane B without identity loss.

### What Year 2 looks like if a deal closes

- 6-12 month founder retention with vesting (assume 80% earned over 18 months)
- OSS core moves to Lictor Foundation (incorporated Apr 2027)
- Commercial features + brand + Dor + (probably) 1-2 hires integrate into acquirer
- Year 2 narrative becomes: "Lictor joined [acquirer] to scale category-defining work"

### Honest caveats

- Most informational acquirer meetings DON'T lead to deals within 12 months
- Pre-IPO acquirers (Snyk) defer everything until post-IPO; if their IPO slips, Lictor's deal slips
- "Strategic partnership" with deferred acquisition option is the more-common outcome than an actual acquisition
- A bad-fit acquisition is worse than no acquisition — Foundation independence + brand-voice survival are non-negotiable

## Lane B — Independent scaling (soft-to-strong fit, no acquirer pressure)

**Trigger:** any of Reality A/B WITHOUT a clear acquirer signal. Most likely outcome of Year 1.

### Year 2 thesis

Lictor scales as an indie cybersec brand to $200k-1M ARR by May 2028. That's an unsexy outcome by VC standards and the *right* outcome for an OSS-led security business.

Math: $30k MRR by end of Year 1 + ~5% MoM compound growth for 12 months ≈ $54k MRR by end of Year 2 = $650k ARR. Plus AaaS + Course + Enterprise — easily $750k-1M ARR if growth holds.

### The 4 levers Year 2 pulls

1. **Add the first paid human hire** (Q3 2027 — dev advocate OR community manager — pick based on what's most bottlenecked). $80-120k all-in. Year 1's revenue funds this.

2. **Scale Audit-as-a-Service from 20 customers to 100** — the highest-margin revenue stream. By Q2 2028, AaaS is half of total revenue. Sales motion: VC firms auditing their portfolios, agencies auditing their clients, OSS-friendly enterprises auditing their AI surfaces.

3. **Launch Lictor Studio Pro (~$79 one-time or $7/mo per seat)** — Q3 2027. Pro features: encrypted local audit history, custom checks, team licenses, priority support. Aligns with the existing Studio v0.2 spec. Easy 50-100x of free-tier downloads-to-paid.

4. **Course graduates a Year-1 cohort of 500 → Year-2 cohort of 1,500** — pricing rises to $149 then $199 as track record builds. Cohort 2 launches Q3 2027 with the alumni testimonials from Cohort 1.

### Year 2 milestones (Lane B)

| Quarter | Headline | Revenue target |
|---|---|---|
| Q3 2027 (Jun-Aug) | First hire onboarded; Studio Pro ships | $35-50k MRR |
| Q4 2027 (Sep-Nov) | AaaS hits 50 customers; Course Cohort 2 enrolled | $50-70k MRR |
| Q1 2028 (Dec-Feb) | Lictor Foundation fully governing OSS; first international event sponsorship | $60-85k MRR |
| Q2 2028 (Mar-May) | Year 2 retro: $70-100k MRR achieved, decision point on Lane B→C jump | $70-100k MRR |

### What Lane B chooses against

- VC fundraise (Lane C) — Lane B intentionally avoids dilution
- Aggressive enterprise sales (more than 5-10 deals/yr) — that's a different operating shape; needs SDR + AE hires
- Pivot to compliance certification (SOC2-as-a-Service, etc.) — that's an entirely different product category
- Geographic expansion beyond translations + 1 conference per region

The Lane B philosophy: *boring compounding > heroic acceleration*.

## Lane C — Fundraise + scale (acquirer interest IS the validation, not the outcome)

**Trigger:** Reality A + multiple acquirer signals + Dor genuinely wants to grow Lictor 5-10x bigger over 5 years rather than sell.

### Year 2 thesis

Lictor takes a $5-10M seed/Series A at $30-60M post-money to fund 6-10 hires over 18 months. Outcome target: $5-10M ARR by Year 5 OR a much bigger exit at Year 5+.

### The strategic case FOR fundraising

- Vibe-coder market is a winner-take-most category by Year 5; first-mover with capital wins distribution permanently
- Multiple platform partnerships need dedicated BD work + engineering resources
- Enterprise revenue requires real sales motion + customer success — that's 3-4 hires alone
- The acquirer pool grows: post-Series A, more acquirers are interested (validated by an institutional investor's diligence)
- Personal upside: founder ownership at exit is higher in absolute dollars even with 25% dilution

### The strategic case AGAINST

- Lictor is OSS-led; investors expect 30%+ ARR growth which is hard with OSS-led GTM
- Solo founder + 15-agent crew → 10-person team is a *cultural* transition, not just operational
- The acquirer-readiness position weakens for "small strategic" acquirers (Snyk doesn't want to acquire a Series A startup; they want to acquire a brand + team)
- Most OSS security startups that raise Series A don't outperform the ones that don't — Aikido is the standout that did; most disappear

### The Lane C playbook (IF chosen)

**Q3 2027:**
- Engage a partner-level intro at one specific firm (a16z, Bessemer, Founders Fund, Sequoia, Ten Eleven Ventures — pick by relationship + sector focus)
- Run a focused pitch process: 4-6 firms, parallel, 60 days. NOT 20 firms shotgun.
- Term sheet target: $5-8M at $30-50M post

**Q4 2027:**
- Close round, ideally with 1-2 strategic angels (cybersec veterans) on cap table
- First 3 hires: dev advocate, founding engineer #2, head of sales OR head of community (depending on which Lane B lever was strongest)

**Q1 2028:**
- 3 more hires: founding engineer #3, customer success lead, content/marketing lead
- Enterprise sales motion formalized
- Lictor Foundation incorporated (regardless of fundraise)

**Q2 2028:**
- Team of 7-10, revenue at $1-2M ARR run-rate, growth at 15-25% MoM
- Board cadence established; quarterly board meetings; standard SaaS operating cadence

### Honest caveats

- Lane C requires Dor's psychological readiness to manage people, run board meetings, share equity, and *let go of solo control*
- Series A founders rarely re-enter "build mode"; they become operators. If Dor wants to keep coding, Lane B is better.
- The institutional money clock is 5-7 years; you commit to an exit window
- 80% of Series A AI security companies will be dead or merged by 2032; Lane C is the highest-variance path

## If Reality C (Year 1 weak fit)

Honest pivot options:

1. **The category is real but Lictor's positioning was wrong.** Rebrand, reposition, ship Year 1.5 (a 6-month sprint with the corrected positioning). Burn another $30-50k cash. Run another 6-month evaluation.

2. **The category is real but Lictor is too late.** Snyk / Aikido / GitHub took it. Wind down Lictor as an open-source project (still useful for the OSS community), return to GenerationAI consulting full-time, fold Lictor's brand into consulting positioning.

3. **The category is wrong.** Vibe-coders genuinely don't pay for security. Hard pivot: take the agent crew + framework Lictor built and apply to a different vertical (compliance evidence automation? AI risk management for enterprises?). Use the year's learnings; keep the infrastructure; kill the brand.

Option 1 is the most-common pivot. Option 3 is the deepest reset.
