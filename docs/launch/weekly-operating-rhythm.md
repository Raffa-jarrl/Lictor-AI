# Lictor weekly operating rhythm

> **Generated:** 2026-05-15
> **Purpose:** turn the 12-month year plan into actual recurring habits. Plans without rhythm fail. This is the metronome.
> **Who runs it:** Raffa + the 11-agent crew (15-agent crew from Q1 2027). Meerkat agent + Mantis agent enforce the cadence.
> **When to deviate:** rarely. If you skip Monday's prep twice in 4 weeks, Mantis escalates to Wolf and you reset the rhythm before continuing.

---

## The week at a glance

```
MON  Plan + brief         Wolf's briefing read; week scoped
TUE  TEARDOWN PUBLISHED   The single most important day of the week
WED  Create               Record video, draft post, write skill
THU  Engage               Community, email, partner conversations
FRI  Review + ship        Mantis's review, weekly newsletter, next week's prep
SAT  Off                  No work. Real off, not "light Slack."
SUN  Light strategic      Read, think, no deliverables
```

The whole rhythm hinges on Tuesday's teardown publish. Everything else feeds that or follows from it.

## Monday — Plan + brief

### Goal
Walk into the week with a clear ship list. Surface any drift from the year plan before it compounds.

### Morning (60 minutes)

```
09:00  Read Wolf's daily briefing (briefings/YYYY-MM-DD.json)
        Look for: pipeline failures, customer-ready items, agent escalations
09:15  Read this month's section in year-plan-2026-2027.md
        Compare: where are we vs where the plan says we should be?
09:30  Read Mongoose's weekly competitive_drifts (if Sunday's scan produced any)
        Note: any critical drift = action this week, not next
09:45  Set the week's TOP 3 things in tasks.jsonl (status: this-week)
        Rule: 3 maximum. Add a 4th only by removing one.
10:00  Read Mantis's weekly review (last Friday's output)
        What pattern is drifting? What recommendation is unimplemented?
```

### Afternoon (90 minutes)

```
14:00  Email triage — Inbox Zero attempt
        Meerkat agent has pre-triaged GitHub issues. Skim Meerkat's queue.
14:30  Respond to the 5 most-important threads (no batching for later)
15:00  Block calendar for the week's deep work
        Tue: teardown publish + Twitter thread (4h block)
        Wed: video recording + content drafting (4h block)
        Thu: community + partner calls (variable)
        Fri: review + ship + next-week prep (3h block)
```

### Hard rule for Monday
**No code. No video recording. No new commitments.** Monday is for planning + decisions. The week will fall apart if you build before you plan.

## Tuesday — TEARDOWN PUBLISHED

### Goal
Publish this week's teardown across all 10 surfaces. Single most important day of the week.

### Pre-Tuesday checklist (Mon evening or Tue morning)

By the time you sit down Tuesday morning, the following are READY:

- Lyrebird's draft teardown report (drafted Friday, reviewed Monday by Mantis)
- The companion Twitter thread (6 tweets, drafted by Bat + Bee)
- The LinkedIn post (drafted in Raffa's voice, distinct framing)
- The HN submission text (Show HN or text post, no link to landing page — content-first)
- The Substack newsletter (the teardown's writeup formatted for newsletter audience)
- The 60-second short video (Peacock agent drafted Sunday; record Monday afternoon if needed)
- The YouTube long-form video (recorded the previous Friday/Saturday; edited Sun-Mon)

### Tuesday execution

```
09:00  Final 30-min read-through of the teardown report
        Catch any embarrassing typo. Verify disclosure timeline.
09:30  Publish teardown to lictorai.com/teardowns/YYYY-MM-DD-slug
        Use Bee's landing page template; ensure SEO meta tags correct
10:00  Post the YouTube long-form video (set as "scheduled" for 12:00 PT
        for east-coast viewing window)
10:15  Post the LinkedIn version
10:30  Submit to Hacker News (target 09:10 PT for east-coast voting window)
10:45  Post the Twitter thread (use a thread tool — Hypefury or similar)
11:00  Newsletter scheduled for Tue 14:00 PT delivery via Substack
11:30  Post the short to TikTok / IG Reels / YouTube Shorts (Peacock-drafted)
12:00  Reddit submission to r/SaaS (organic; never spam-shaped)
13:00  LUNCH — actual lunch, away from screen
14:00  Monitor first-3-hours engagement; respond to early comments
        on HN, Twitter, LinkedIn. Don't be on every platform — pick 2.
17:00  End of Tuesday. Day's work shipped.
```

### What gets sacrificed on Tuesday
Code, meetings, calls, deep email. Tuesday is reserved.

If a teardown is not ready to publish Tuesday, you don't publish Wednesday — you publish next Tuesday. The cadence is the asset.

### When Tuesday fails
If the cadence breaks (no teardown published 2 weeks in a row), Mantis escalates to Wolf, who emails Raffa with: "the engine has stalled, here's the diagnostic, here's the fix path." The 2-week buffer matters because life happens. The 4-week buffer = the engine is broken.

## Wednesday — Create

### Goal
Produce next week's content. The Tuesday rhythm only works if Wednesday refills the bucket.

### Block 1 — Video (10:00–13:00)

```
10:00  Read this week's Peacock script (long-form, ~10-min)
        Customize beats with this week's actual teardown findings
10:30  Light + mic + camera setup (15 min)
10:45  Record long-form in 2-3 takes. Don't aim for perfect.
11:30  Record 2 shorts (60s each)
12:00  Quick edit pass (cut filler, no fancy effects)
13:00  Upload long-form to YouTube as "private," shorts as draft
```

### Block 2 — Writing (14:00–17:00)

```
14:00  Write/edit the cornerstone blog post drafted by Lyrebird
        Sometimes it's a teardown writeup; sometimes a positioning post
15:00  Draft the next week's Twitter thread + LinkedIn post
        Bat + Bee have draft templates; humanize them
16:00  Newsletter for THIS Friday — 30 minutes of writing
16:30  Review Meerkat's GitHub triage queue from earlier in the day
17:00  Wrap.
```

### Hard rule for Wednesday
**Ship something visible by EOD.** Even a single tweet. The day cannot end with zero output.

## Thursday — Engage

### Goal
The single day per week dedicated to humans (not output).

### Morning — Community

```
09:00  Discord / Slack: 60 min of active presence
        Answer questions, welcome new members, surface interesting threads
10:00  GitHub: review the 3 most-thoughtful issues opened this week
        Reply personally; don't let Meerkat handle these
11:00  One in-depth response to a community user's "show me my audit" question
        Treat as content — these often become next week's teardown subject
```

### Afternoon — Partners + press

```
14:00  Partner conversations (Lovable / Bolt / v0 platform teams)
        Even one 15-min Zoom per week compounds over months
15:00  Press follow-ups
        After a major teardown publishes, journalists who didn't bite Tuesday
        may bite Wednesday/Thursday. Keep the relationship warm.
16:00  Customer 1:1 (one paying Teams or AaaS customer per week)
        Pure listening. No upsell. Capture what they actually use.
17:00  Customer-success log entry: what did you learn?
```

### Hard rule for Thursday
**No new code. No new content.** The only deliverables on Thursday are human relationships.

## Friday — Review + ship + prep

### Goal
Mantis's weekly review runs Friday morning. Newsletter ships Friday afternoon. The week wraps deliberately.

### Morning (90 minutes)

```
09:00  Read Mantis's weekly review output (output/review-week-NN.md)
        Mantis has audited the week's content, agent output, customer signals
09:30  Triage Mantis's ONE recommendation
        If it's "tighten voice rules in writer prompt" — task it for next week
        If it's "the engine is producing X false-positive pattern" — fix now
10:00  Update the year-plan tracking: ✅ this week's commitments
        Mark anything that slipped + WHY
10:30  Write a 5-bullet "what landed, what didn't" Slack/Discord post
        Visible internally + externally; signals progress to the community
```

### Afternoon — Newsletter + content prep

```
14:00  Friday newsletter (ships 18:00 PT)
        Built from the week's teardown + 2 community spotlights + a Raffa essay
14:45  Bee has drafted next week's landing page for the teardown subject
        Review + approve
15:00  Bat has drafted next week's Twitter thread + LinkedIn post
        Review + approve
15:30  Peacock has drafted next week's video scripts
        Review + edit for voice
16:00  Lyrebird's draft of next week's teardown report
        Read end-to-end; flag any concerns to Mantis for Mon review
17:00  Wrap. Set up Monday's briefing trigger.
```

### Hard rule for Friday
**Inbox to zero. Slack to zero. Discord to zero.** No open loops crossing into Saturday.

## Saturday — Off

No work. No "quick check" of Slack. No "one more reply" on Twitter.

The agent crew runs autonomously. Saturday is when they fire heartbeats without supervision. If something breaks on Saturday, Wolf escalates to Raffa's phone with an actual high-stakes alert. Otherwise, off.

The discipline matters: founder burnout is the #1 risk in the year plan. Real off-days are the mitigation.

## Sunday — Light strategic

### Goal
Read. Think. No deliverables.

```
10:00  Read 2-3 long-form articles (industry, not Lictor-adjacent)
11:00  Walk + think
14:00  Optional: 1-2 hour deep-think session on a Q4-or-later question
        Examples: pricing model for Studio Pro, the Lictor Foundation thesis,
        the Year 2 plan, etc.
        Write nothing publish-ready; just notes for Monday's brief
```

Sunday is the only day where you can think *forward* of the current week. Resist the urge to do real work. The mistake here is letting Sunday become "Monday-light." It isn't.

- [ ] Adjust the year-plan if reality has shifted materially
- [ ] Outside accountant produces a quarterly financial summary

## When the rhythm breaks

The rhythm WILL break. Expected. The discipline is in the recovery.

### Single-week skip
You miss a Tuesday teardown. Acceptable. Pick up next week. Don't try to "make up" by doing 2 teardowns next week — that breaks the cadence in a different direction.

### Two-week slip
The engine is stalling. Mantis has already escalated to Wolf. Hold an honest 30-min Friday review: what changed? Time? Health? Audience signal? Pick one specific fix for next week.

### Month-long slip
Material problem. Pause public commitments. Email the community honestly: "the cadence broke. Here's why. Here's when it'll restart." Honesty here builds more credibility than pretending you're on track.

### Quarter-long slip
The year plan needs a serious re-evaluation. Schedule a multi-day strategic offsite (even solo). Re-write the plan based on what reality taught you. Don't just resume — re-plan.

## What's NOT in the rhythm

Deliberately excluded:

- **Daily standups** — Raffa is solo. The agent crew has Wolf. No standup needed.
- **Daily metrics checking** — Wednesday and Friday are enough. Daily metric checking is anxiety, not discipline.
- **Twitter doom-scrolling** — feeds compulsiveness, not the operation. Twitter engagement is on a schedule, not on-demand.
- **Daily "quick sync" with anyone** — there's nobody to sync with. Save sync energy for Thursday's intentional engagement.
- **"Open work hours" model** — explicit blocks beat open hours. Open hours = no hours.

## The cumulative effect

If you run this rhythm for 12 months:

- 48 published teardowns (vs the year-plan's 32 — buffer)
- 48 founder videos (target)
- 48 weekly newsletters
- 48 Thursday community-engagement sessions
- 48 Friday Mantis reviews
- 48 weeks of compounding outbound

That's the year. It's mostly the same Monday over and over, with the content + product slowly hardening underneath. The "outcomes" people see at the end — 50k stars, $25-50k MRR, category-defining brand — are the visible tip of this rhythm.

Boring. On purpose. The boring is the moat.
