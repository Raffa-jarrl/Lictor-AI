# Lictor M&A Strategy — 12-Month Plan to May 2027

**Frame:** Optionality, not commitment. By May 2027 Lictor should be a credible buy for 4-6 plausible acquirers AND a viable independent company. Closing either path is the failure mode.

---

## 1. Recent OSS-led security M&A (2023-2026)

The window was the hottest since 2021, driven by AI inflection and consolidation pressure. What matters for Lictor isn't the Wiz megadeals but the tier below — strategic tuck-ins in the $150M-$1B range.

**Mega-deals (set the comp ceiling):**
- **Google → Wiz** ($32B, announced Mar 2025, closed Mar 2026). ~30x forward ARR on ~$1B ARR.
- **Cisco → Splunk** ($28B, closed Mar 2024). Observability-into-security.
- **IBM → HashiCorp** ($6.4B, closed Feb 2025). OSS-led, instructive comp.
- **ServiceNow → Armis** ($7.75B, Dec 2025) + Moveworks ($2.85B) — $11.6B on security in 2025.
- **Palo Alto → CyberArk** (~$25B, closed Feb 2026, 26% premium).

**Strategic tuck-ins (the real Lictor comp set):**
- **Fortinet → Lacework** ($152M cash, Aug 2024). Raised $1.3B+ at $8.3B peak — distressed write-down. Downside reference.
- **Palo Alto → Talon** ($625M, late 2023, ~18 months old at acquisition) and **Dig Security** (~$400M).
- **CrowdStrike** spree: Flow ($200M), Adaptive Shield ($300M), Bionic ($350-500M), Onum ($290M), Pangea ($260M, API/AI), SGNL ($740M, Jan 2026), Seraphic ($420M).
- **GitLab → Oxeye** ($30-40M, Mar 2024). The most Lictor-shaped comp by size.
- **Snyk → Helios** (Jan 2024), **Probely** (Nov 2024, DAST), **Invariant Labs** (Jun 2025, agentic AI security — ETH spinoff, <1 year old).
- **Aikido → Allseek + Haicker** (Sep 2025, AI pen-testing).
- **SentinelOne → Prompt Security** ($180M), **Observo AI** ($225M).

**Read-through:** Snyk → Invariant Labs is the single most important comp. ETH spinoff, founded 2024, acquired mid-2025 to plant a flag in agentic AI security. That's the playbook — be the named asset in a category an acquirer urgently needs to claim.

## 2. Acquirer landscape (May 2027 view)

Ranked by realistic likelihood. 1-5 where 3 = "plausible if Lictor executes."

**1. Snyk — 4/5.** Most natural acquirer. Building the "AI Trust Platform," already bought Invariant Labs to claim agentic AI security, pattern of buying brand-defining OSS teams (DeepCode, Probely). Pays for: category position + Apache 2.0 OSS audience + the vibe-coder narrative they lack. Multiple: 12-20x ARR; acqui-hire $5-15M if narrative hasn't landed. **Risk:** They build internally, or Aikido beats them. Snyk IPO timing matters — pre-IPO they hoard cash, post-IPO they spend.

**2. Aikido — 3/5.** $1B valuation Jan 2026, $60M Series B, aggressive on AI tuck-ins. European peer. Pays for: US developer brand, OSS distribution, vibe-coder audience. Multiple: 8-15x ARR, more likely stock+cash. **Risk:** They build Bolt/Lovable integration themselves; Belgian buyer culture is cost-conscious.

**3. GitHub / Microsoft — 3/5.** Distribution play. GitHub bought Dependabot at $14k MRR in 2019 — relevant precedent. Pays for: developer mindshare, "Lictor for Copilot/Codespaces" bundle. Multiple: talent-driven, $20-80M acqui-hire unless real ARR. **Risk:** GitHub Advanced Security covers adjacent surface; vibe-coders may be out-of-scope.

**4. Cloudflare — 3/5.** Bought Replicate (Nov 2025), Astro (Jan 2026), Human Native (Jan 2026). Seven prior cybersec acquisitions. Pays for: developer credibility + AI-app-security next to Workers AI. Multiple: 10-15x ARR. **Risk:** Security DNA is network-edge, not app-layer.

**5. Datadog — 2/5.** Advanced talks to buy Upwind for $1B (Jul 2025), shipped Bits AI security agents 2026. Pays for: AI-app-observability story. Multiple: 15-25x for hot assets. **Risk:** Prefers revenue-mature targets; solo builders aren't their ICP.

**6. Anthropic — 2/5.** Bought Bun (Dec 2025) for Claude Code. $100M committed to AI security including OSS donations. Pays for: Claude Code skill ecosystem, AI-safety cred, vibe-coder narrative. Multiple: strategic/talent — $30-150M acqui-hire. **Risk:** Signal is fund OSS, not buy it. Partnership more likely than acquisition.

**7. OpenAI — 2/5.** Similar logic, different surface (Codex). Pays for: safer code-gen outputs. Multiple: $30-150M strategic. **Risk:** Weaker M&A muscle; tends to hire individuals.

**8. GitLab — 2/5.** Oxeye precedent ($30-40M, 2024) shows exact deal shape. Pays for: SAST/AppSec extension into AI-app territory. Multiple: 8-12x ARR. **Risk:** Weak stock, shrinking M&A appetite, build-internally bias.

**9. Vanta / Drata — 2/5.** Vanta $4.15B valuation, $300M ARR, +69% YoY. Drata $100M+ ARR. Pays for: AI-app trust story for compliance buyer. Multiple: 10-15x ARR. **Risk:** Compliance-first DNA; security tooling is a stretch.

**10. CrowdStrike / Palo Alto / SentinelOne — 2/5.** Enterprise incumbents. Write $200M-$750M checks but want $20M+ ARR. Multiple: 10-20x ARR. **Risk:** Lictor below threshold; needs 30-50x revenue scale first.

**Below threshold:** Atlassian (1/5, wrong DNA — productivity not security), HashiCorp/IBM and Fortinet (1/5, in 18-24 month integration digestion).

## 3. Valuation framework

Honest numbers. OSS founders dream of 50x; reality in 2025-2026 was 15.2x average private cybersec, 16.3x M&A, with cloud security peaking 21.7x average / 35.5x outlier (Finro). May 2027 macro unknowable — restrictive rates compress to 8-15x; AI hype sustains 15-25x for hot assets.

- **Pre-revenue / brand-only ($0-300k ARR):** Acqui-hire shape. $5-30M total, mostly acquirer equity, 2-4 year vesting. Snyk → Invariant Labs sits here. **Lictor today.**
- **$1-5M ARR (strategic-tech buys):** $20-100M range, 10-25x ARR. GitLab → Oxeye ($30-40M) is canonical. **Realistic May 2027 target if Lictor hits $1-2M ARR.**
- **$5-25M ARR (real product buys):** $100-500M, 15-25x. Palo Alto → Dig ($400M), CrowdStrike → Pangea ($260M), SentinelOne → Prompt Security ($180M). Not 12-month-reachable from current ARR.
- **$25M+ ARR (full-product buys):** $500M-$2B+, 12-20x. Snyk-class. Not relevant.

**Macro caveat:** Wiz pulled forward a year of cybersec M&A optimism. By May 2027, integration overhang plus any rate shift could compress multiples 30-40%. Plan assuming **10-15x ARR mid-case**, not 25-30x headlines.

## 4. The Lictor-specific positioning play (12 months to May 2027)

Ordered by leverage:

**Brand searchability inside acquirers.** When their strategy team searches "AI app security" or "Lovable/Bolt/v0 security" internally, Lictor must be the first result. Own specific terms: ship the teardown engine publicly, get cited by Krebs/DarkReading/The Register, land 2026 Gartner/Forrester analyst notes — even as a cool-kid call-out. One analyst mention beats six months of outreach.

**Multiple acquirer signals, deliberately cultivated.** Never depend on one buyer. Track: GitHub stars from acquirer employees (visible), conference talks at acquirer events, integration requests from their product teams. Target 3+ active signals by Q1 2027. One interested acquirer = price-taker.

**Clean books from day one.** Separate Lictor revenue from GenerationAI consulting **now**. Lictor entity, bank account, Stripe, cap table. Commingled books cost 20-40% off any deal.

**Documentation as a moat.** Every customer interaction logged. Every architectural decision in an ADR. Every agent in the 11-AI crew documented (purpose, prompts, evals). Diligence-ready companies sell for 1.3-1.8x more.

**IP clean-room.** Apache 2.0 is acquirer-friendly. Get a CLA in place before contributor count grows. Solo founder = zero employee IP assignment ambiguity, which is rare and valuable. If hiring a single contractor, get IP assignment signed before any commit.

**Inbound, not outbound.** Founder-led outbound collapses valuations 40-60%. Build the inbound funnel: high-signal content, public design partnerships with named brands, analyst briefings, keynotes. May 2027 goal: "their M&A teams have already discussed Lictor internally."

**Diligence pack, pre-built.** Build a "Diligence" Drive folder in month one. Populate monthly: financial statements (cash basis is fine), customer list with logos and contract terms, OSS license audit, CLA status, all IP assignments, security audit (SOC 2 Type I minimum), pen-test results, third-party dependency + license list, churn data, clean commit history.

## 5. The "stay independent" alternative

Optionality ≠ commitment. The strongest M&A position is one where you can credibly walk away.

**The $5-25M ARR path:** Lictor as a category-defining OSS security suite — 5-15k paying customers at $50-200/mo self-serve plus 50-200 enterprise contracts at $10-50k/yr. Achievable in 4-5 years from current ARR at 80-120% NRR. Comps: Tailscale, Cal.com, Posthog.

**Requires:** Series A by mid-2027 ($8-15M at $40-80M post), real GTM hire (sales-led enterprise), customer success, SOC 2 Type II / ISO 27001, and 5-7 more years of building. The 11-AI-agent crew works to $5-50M ARR; beyond that requires traditional hiring.

**Trade-offs:** Independence trades a $30-150M liquidity event for a 5x-larger eventual outcome at 3-5x the time and 10x the execution risk. ~60% of OSS companies at this stage never reach $10M ARR.

**Right May 2027 frame:** "We have two credible inbound acquisition conversations at 15x+ ARR AND a Series A term sheet at $50M+ post." Engineer toward that. The work to get there — clean books, brand, documentation, inbound signals — is identical to acquisition prep. Both paths, same 12 months.
