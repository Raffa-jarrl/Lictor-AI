## 6. The five talking-point arcs

What Raffa expects to be asked, with a 1–2 sentence answer and supporting facts. Use these to prep an interview or to verify a quote.

### Arc 1 — "What does Lictor do that Snyk doesn't?"

**Answer:** "Snyk built for the Fortune 500 CISO with a five-developer floor and a compliance budget. Lictor built for the solo founder shipping a Lovable app on Saturday — free, local, plain-English, no signup. We're not a cheaper Snyk. We're the other product."

**Supporting facts:**
- Snyk's Team tier starts at $25/dev/month with a five-developer minimum (snyk.io/plans). Solo founders are structurally excluded by pricing design.
- Snyk's `agent-scan` requires a Snyk account and sends agent data to Snyk's API for analysis. Lictor runs 100% locally — no token, no telemetry, no cloud round-trip on the audit path.
- Zero mention of Lovable, Bolt, or v0 across snyk.io/platform, evo.ai.snyk.io, or Snyk's March 2026 Agent Security launch. They have ceded the vibe-coder beachhead.

### Arc 2 — "Why open source?"

**Answer:** "Trust in security tools should be verifiable. Apache 2.0 means every check is a markdown file you can read, every agent is a SOUL file you can fork, and the commitment doesn't depend on a certificate or a contract. Open source is how we make the moat the community can defend with us."

**Supporting facts:**
- Apache 2.0 license across the entire suite — `core`, `shield`, `sentinel`, `sentinel-py`, `guardian`, `skills`.
- Lictor Foundation incorporation planned Q2 2027 (501(c)(3) or equivalent) — structural commitment that the core never gets EE-only features.
- Public CLA, CONTRIBUTING.md, ROADMAP.md, and SECURITY.md from day one. Pull requests merged from external contributors, not just issues acknowledged.

### Arc 3 — "What's the business model?"

**Answer:** "Four streams, all built on the open-source core. Teams subscription at $19/month flat-rate. A $99 course called GenerationAI. Audit-as-a-Service for agencies and VC portfolios. Enterprise pilots when someone deploys it on-prem. No venture capital, no funding round, no dilution — funded by Raffa's consulting income while the OSS compounds."

**Supporting facts:**
- Target: $25–50k MRR by Q2 2027 across all four streams.
- No outside investors. No board. No funding round planned. Self-funded.
- Teams pricing is flat-rate, not per-seat — explicitly chosen to undercut the per-seat motion of incumbents.

### Arc 4 — "Aren't there enough security tools already?"

**Answer:** "There are plenty of security tools for software written by humans for humans on five-person teams. There are almost none for software written by AI for solo founders who don't read JavaScript. AI-built apps are a new category with a new failure mode — hallucinated packages, missing RLS policies, leaked Supabase keys in JS bundles. Generic SAST doesn't pattern-match those because it wasn't built to."

**Supporting facts:**
- 40–62% of AI-generated code ships with security vulnerabilities (multiple academic studies cited in Lictor's blog archive).
- 91.5% of vibe-coded apps had at least one AI-hallucination flaw in Q1 2026.
- Lovable exposed 18,000 users across 170+ databases in a single February 2026 incident.
- Lictor's Radar agent is trained on 1,430+ real audits of vibe-coded apps, tuned for the specific failure patterns generic SAST misses.

### Arc 5 — "What's the worst-case scenario for Lictor?"

**Answer:** "Two scenarios. Snyk ships a free vibe-coder tier and tries to occupy the wedge from above. Or Anthropic ships native code-audit features in Claude and removes the surface entirely. We've documented both in public playbooks. Short version: if Snyk follows, we win on plain English and contribution-open community OSS, which they can't ship without breaking their enterprise motion. If Anthropic ships native audit, Lictor pivots to the orchestration layer above it — the workflow, the teardown archive, the community, the standards work."

**Supporting facts:**
- The full anti-Snyk-free-tier playbook is published as a strategic doc in the public repo (`docs/launch/anti-snyk-free-tier-playbook.md`). No hidden contingency.
- Plan B for native LLM audit features is on the year roadmap (Q4 2026 doc target).
- AUDIT.json — an open standard proposal — is part of the standards-ownership play. Lictor invites Snyk, Aikido, Semgrep, and VibeEval to co-sign.

## 10. Pre-approved teardown coverage

For journalists writing about a specific Lictor teardown.

- **Every teardown writeup is freely re-publishable.** The teardown text, code snippets, finding summaries, and remediation steps are all Apache 2.0 (consistent with the rest of the repo). Quote at any length. Excerpt the full piece if your outlet's licensing allows.
- **The founder of the audited app is owed a separate contact.** Before publishing your piece, reach out to the founder of the application Lictor audited (named in every teardown writeup) for their comment. They have already gone through Lictor's 14-day disclosure process — they are aware the public writeup exists. They deserve the chance to speak for themselves on your piece.
- **Specific findings can be quoted freely.** Snippets of code from the original (vulnerable) application are reproduced in the teardown under fair-use review; you may re-publish them in editorial coverage. Exploit code — proof-of-concept payloads, full attack chains, working exploit scripts — is never published by Lictor. If you need exploit code for an investigative story, email `press@lictor-ai.com` with a written request describing your outlet, your editor, and the security justification. We respond within 48 hours.
- **Best practice:** link to `lictor-ai.com/teardowns/[slug]` rather than scraping the writeup. Our pages stay updated as new information surfaces (e.g., when the audited founder responds, when the issue is patched, when the disclosure is closed). Scraped copies go stale.

If your teardown coverage will be uncomplimentary to Lictor (i.e., you've found a flaw in our methodology, a missed finding, or a false positive), we would still rather read your piece before publication than after. Email `press@lictor-ai.com`. Right-of-reply applies.
